Space content that goes beyond "here's the ISS" and rewards a fascination (maybe things to add to the space tab of daily):

  Central conceit worth building around
     
  Ishimura Bridge Viewport - A single page that treats the Ishimura as if it's a real ship somewhere in
  real space. It has a fictional but consistent position (drifting through the Aegis system), and
  everything else on the page is real space data contextualized through the ship's instruments:
  
  - Distance/time to Earth (with comm-delay countdown for message sends)
  - Which real spacecraft are nearest (Voyager 1/2, JWST, Perseverance, etc)
  - Live space weather affecting the ship
  - Astronomical events visible from the ship's position
  - What Earth thinks it's watching right now (JWST current target)
  
  This gives you one coherent narrative frame that everything else plugs into.
  
  Live/dynamic (not just displays)

  1. Deep space companions - Voyager 1, Voyager 2, Pioneer 10/11, New Horizons, JWST current position +
  distance + one-way comm delay. Updated hourly.
  2. Voyager Golden Record player - Actually play the record's audio: the greetings in 55 languages,
  whale songs, Chuck Berry, the "sounds of Earth." Our species' introduction.
  3. Aurora forecast for your latitude - NOAA Kp index processed through your geo. Ntfy pushes when
  tonight's chance > threshold.
  4. Solar storm early warning - CME detection → themed "SOLAR EVENT INBOUND" alert overlay + technical
  details.
  5. Space weather report - Sun spot count, solar wind speed, X-ray flux. Presented as Ishimura RIG
  readouts.
  6. Currently overhead - Real satellites/spacecraft transiting above you right now. Filter naked-eye
  visible.
  7. Live JWST target - What's the telescope observing at this moment. Small "the eye is looking at NGC
  604 - a star nursery 2.7 million ly away."
  8. Kilonova/supernova alert wire - Rare but epic. Push notification when GRB/kilonova is detected.
  9. Meteor shower calendar + ntfy - 24h before Perseid peak, cross-post to Discord + push. Best viewing
   coords for your location.
  10. Sky right now - Interactive chart of what's above your horizon. If you rp: "Ishimura viewport
  view."
 
  Story / editorial depth
 
  11. Cosmic calendar - Big Bang on Jan 1, humans arriving Dec 31 at 11:58 PM. Daily zoom shows where we
   are today - "3 seconds before midnight." Rotates daily commentary.
  12. Failed mission memorial - Interactive timeline of every lost spacecraft. Beautiful and
  melancholic. Curated writeups for each.
  13. Fermi paradox rotation - Weekly deep-dive essay. Great Filter, Dark Forest, zoo hypothesis, rare
  Earth, self-destruction, simulation. Real theories with commentary.
  14. Exoplanet weekly spotlight - Featured planet, its facts, what we speculate, ESI ranking.
  15. Cold War space race deep-dive - Interactive Sputnik → Apollo. Long-form.
  16. Extreme records wall - Fastest object we've launched, oldest light observed, hottest thing
  measured. Curated fascinating facts.
  17. Cosmic distance ladder explainer - How do we know Andromeda is 2.5M ly away? Each rung explained
  with visualizations.
  18. Prediction tracker (snarky but real) - "Musk said humans on Mars by X. Actual state: Y." Every
  failed prediction of the space age, running scoreboard.
  19. Kyne's notes on the stars - In-universe Dr. Kyne wrote about specific constellations. Fake journal
   entries alongside real astronomy.

  Interactive / personal

  20. Personal spaceship berth - Every crew member randomly assigned a real spacecraft as their "sister
  ship." Their profile page shows its status, current position, comm delay. "Your berth: Voyager 1.
  Currently 24.5 billion km from home."
  21. Star adoption - Each user assigned (or picks) a real cataloged star from Gaia. Their coordinates,
  distance, mass, spectral type. Appears on their RIG profile. Now they have a spot in the sky.
  22. Namesake watchtower - Watch for real events at "your" star (occultations, novae, brightening).
  23. Cosmic address - "You are at Local Group / Milky Way / Orion Arm / 8kpc from center / 0.4pc from
  Sirius / L1.98AU from Sol." Reduces user to a coordinate.
  24. Launch companion mode - When a rocket goes up, full-immersive page: countdown, live stream,
  telemetry embed, mission profile. Turns launches into events on the site.
  25. JWST image of the moment - Latest release, with expert-summary generated by cheap LLM. Zoomable,
  annotated.
  
  Novel/weird

  26. Nearest known black hole - Direction + distance from Earth right now. Its mass, spin, temperature.
   Interactive orientation.
  27. Deep field zoom - Hubble Ultra Deep + JWST Deep Field. Interactive zoom - click any smudge, get
  its identity/redshift/distance.
  28. Radio noise of the sky - Live SDR sample if you set up an SDR receiver, otherwise archived
  recordings from Green Bank/FAST. Actual audio of the universe.
  29. CMB visualizer - Interactive cosmic microwave background with hot/cold spots annotated. That's the
   oldest thing you can see.
  30. Cosmic voice mail - Historical significant radio transmissions from Earth (Arecibo message,
  Voyager Golden Record, KEO). Where they've traveled to now.
  31. Where would you die - "You entered Jupiter's atmosphere. At 12,000 miles down you're crushed by
  100 million atmospheres." Educational + morbid.
  32. Wow signal + BLC-1 - Cataloged anomalies. What we know, what we don't. Ongoing investigation
  status.

  Data-source suggestions (real APIs)
  
  - NASA API (planetary, APOD, mars imagery, NeoWs) - free with key
  - Launch Library 2 - all launches, past + future
  - Space-Track / N2YO - satellite/spacecraft positions
  - NOAA SWPC - space weather, aurora forecasts
  - JPL Horizons - precise spacecraft ephemerides
  - exoplanet.eu / NASA Exoplanet Archive - exoplanet data
  - HYG database - star catalog (10k+ stars)
  - CalSky, in-the-sky.org - astronomical events
  - Green Bank / FAST archived data - radio astronomy
  - Wikimedia + NASA image galleries - free imagery
